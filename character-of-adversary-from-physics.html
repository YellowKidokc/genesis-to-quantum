From Information Theory

The Character of the Lie

  • Kolmogorov complexity: every truth has a minimum description length
  • A lie ABOUT a truth must encode BOTH the false version AND suppress the true version
  • Maintaining one lie is cheap. Maintaining a NETWORK of lies costs exponentially more
  • Shannon: a noisy channel carries less signal per bit
  • A channel carrying deception is carrying noise disguised as signal
  • Landauer: erasing information costs energy. Suppressing truth IS information erasure.

There is a constraint on deception that is as fundamental as the speed of light. You cannot lie for free. Every suppressed truth costs energy. Every maintained deception costs complexity. And the costs COMPOUND — not linearly, but exponentially — as the web of deception grows.

A single lie can be simpler than a complex truth. “The earth is flat” is six syllables. The geodetic description of the oblate spheroid is a textbook.

But a SYSTEM of lies — a complete alternative reality where every lie is consistent with every other lie and none of them touch the truth — that is thermodynamically impossible to maintain indefinitely. The costs grow faster than the resources.
