03 · The Patterns
Gravity

The weakest force in nature. A hundred thousand trillion trillion times weaker than electromagnetism. And yet it shaped every galaxy. It never spikes. It never rushes. It reaches everywhere. You can't block it. It doesn't push — it curves the path so that everything, if you stop fighting it, bends toward center.

I thought: that's patience. Written in geometry. Not like patience. Patience.

The Strong Force

The strongest force in existence. The harder you try to pull two quarks apart, the stronger it gets. Slack inside the bond — total freedom. But try to leave? The universe would rather create new matter than let the bond break.

I thought: that's love.

Light

Sustains itself. Needs no medium, no carrier, no permission. A photon from 13 billion years ago arrives with its original information intact. It travels at maximum speed. Nothing is faster. And you cannot refuse it — if it touches your retina, you see.

I thought: that's truth.

Entropy

The universal tendency toward disorder. Things fall apart. Relationships fracture. Systems decay. Every closed system trends toward chaos. Nothing is exempt.

I thought: that's sin. Described by physics 3,000 years after Moses described it in words.

These aren't metaphors.

A metaphor is when you say one thing is like another thing. But these patterns have the same mathematical structure on both sides. Same equations. Same boundary behaviors. Same conservation laws. Same symmetry properties. Different variable names.

The question stopped being "is this interesting?" and started being "is this true?"
