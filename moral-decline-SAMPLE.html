Abstract

This series presents a systematic, data-driven analysis of America's moral, institutional, and cultural decline from 1958 to 2025. Using the FACTS framework (Family, Accountability, Cognitive integrity, Trust, and Spiritual grounding), we demonstrate that the observed deterioration is not random but follows predictable thermodynamic patterns — specifically, the behavior of a system undergoing an irreversible phase transition.

The evidence spans demographic data, institutional trust surveys, cognitive performance metrics, linguistic drift analysis, and spiritual practice indicators across seven decades. The conclusion is stark: America has crossed multiple critical thresholds from which historical precedent suggests no civilization has recovered.

Critical Thresholds

1962
Engel v. Vitale — Supreme Court removes prayer from public schools. The first domino. Within one generation, every FACTS metric begins declining.
1973
Roe v. Wade — Sanctity of life redefined by judicial fiat. Accelerates the separation of moral consequence from personal choice.
1995
Internet Mass Adoption — Information decentralization begins. Within 15 years, attention spans halve and algorithmic radicalization emerges.
2015
Social Media Saturation — Semantic collapse accelerates. Words like "violence," "safety," and "justice" lose fixed meaning. Public discourse fragments.
