## The Social Psychology of Political Tribalism

The foundation of the partisan divide lies in social identity theory (Tajfel & Turner, 1979). Three stages: categorization, identification, comparison. Once "us" and "them" exist, tribalism takes over — members defend the tribe's core ideas without objective reflection. A fact that implies "us" is wrong and "them" is right gets rejected to preserve psychological security.

## The Perception Gap (KILL SHOT DATA)

More in Common (2019): As education increases among Democrats, their understanding of Republicans becomes LESS ACCURATE.

| Education Level (Democrats) | Accuracy of Republican Views |
|-----------------------------|------------------------------|
| No High School Diploma | ~300% MORE accurate than postgrads |
| Postgraduate Degree | LOWEST accuracy / most distorted view |

Why: Highly educated individuals report the LEAST political disagreement in daily life — deepest social silos. "Herd-minded" culture where bad ideas go unchallenged.

**Framework Translation:** Higher credentialing → deeper silos → increased entropy → degraded signal. Education increases information density locally while DECREASING cross-domain coherence. This is entropy wearing the costume of knowledge.

## The Time Preference Math (ENTROPY IN ECONOMIC FORM)

Subjective rate of time preference (r) — the rate at which people discount future rewards:

| Socioeconomic Background | Time Preference (r) |
|--------------------------|-------------------|
| White, College-Educated, Top 5% | 12% |
| Middle-Class Average | 15% |
| Non-white, Non-College, Bottom 5% | 19% |

r_poor > r_rich — but NOT because the poor are irresponsible. They're responding rationally to a high-noise environment where future "shocks" (job loss, medical bill, car breakdown) are so likely that saving feels futile.

**Framework Translation:** This IS the coherence equation in economic form. High entropy system = shortened coherence horizon. Stable system → 10-year planning window (signal persists). Precarious system → 10-day planning window (noise overwhelms signal). **dχ/dt ∝ -S + G** expressed in dollar signs.

## The Six Narratives (Both Sides, Raw)

### Democrats on Republicans:
"We are trying to build a society that is fair, inclusive, and grounded in science. The other side is obsessed with maintaining a hierarchy that only benefits people like them. They use fear and misinformation to manipulate their base, and they are willing to dismantle democratic institutions just to stay in power."

### Republicans on Democrats:
"We are the defenders of faith, family, and freedom. The other side wants to control everything we say, do, and think. They want to tear down our borders, defund our police, and teach our children that our country is fundamentally evil."

### Urban on Rural:
"They are living in a bubble and refusing to join the modern world. They complain about the government while their towns are kept afloat by the taxes paid by people in the cities."

### Rural on Urban:
"They think they're smarter than us because they have a fancy degree, but they wouldn't know how to fix a tractor or grow a garden if their lives depended on it. They make laws that affect our land without ever setting foot in our counties."

### College-Educated on Non-College:
"They are afraid of ideas that challenge their worldview. Their anti-intellectualism is a danger to our democracy because it leaves them vulnerable to every conspiracy theory and populist lie."

### Non-College on College-Educated:
"They spent four years being told they're better than us. They have plenty of 'book smarts' but zero 'street smarts.' They use their degrees to gatekeep power."

### Wealthy on Poor:
"They stay poor because they can't think ahead. If they just had a little more grit and made better choices, they could get out of the hole they're in."

### Poor on Wealthy:
"They have no idea how expensive it is to be poor. They were born with everything given to them and they have the nerve to call us 'lazy.'"

### Religious on Secular:
"They are trying to build a world with no foundation. They've torn down the family and the church, and now they wonder why everyone is lonely and depressed."

### Secular on Religious:
"They want to impose their ancient myths on everyone else. They use 'faith' as a cover for bigotry. Their 'traditional values' are just a way to keep things the way they've always been—with them in charge."

## Institutional Bias Data

Faculty demographics shift: 1984 liberal-to-conservative ratio was roughly balanced (39% to 34%). By 2018, tenure-track professors at elite institutions were overwhelmingly liberal, some departments with zero registered Republicans.

Ivy League admissions: more students from top 1% of income than from bottom 60%.

92% of "Devoted Conservatives" believe hard work always leads to success — viewing the stability gap as individual effort rather than structural risk.
