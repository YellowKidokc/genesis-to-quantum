## NOTE 1: The Killer — Why People Don't Come (place TBD, possibly after "The Standard")

The reason people don't believe in God a lot of times is moral indignation. They look at the world and say "this doesn't meet MY standards." And they get to rationalize rejecting God by judging the world around them.

But the deal is — when you look at perfect righteousness. A righteousness that doesn't want anything, doesn't need anything, and does everything for you. People don't come to Jesus because they don't want to look at the measure of perfect righteousness and judge THEMSELVES by that measure.

It's daunting. But until you do, you really can't see how dirty you are.

And THAT is the truest definition of grace there is. Because:
- You understand how dirty you are
- You understand how pure He is
- You understand you can never be that way on your own
- You understand the ONLY mechanism to become better is focus on HIM

This might be too much for this article. But it's the terminus. It's where the math lands. Save it for the right moment — maybe "The Equation That Solved Evil" or the article where you show the moral conservation equation's unique solution.

## NOTE 2: The Judging Pattern — Deeper Layer (place after "Same pattern. Six costumes.")

The pattern underneath all six divides isn't just "partial truth becomes tribal badge." There's a deeper layer about WHY humans reach for the badge in the first place.

The behavior is always: point your finger and judge. And the reason we judge on THAT particular thing is because we had a measure of success in it. We judge people on the areas where we're better than them — because it gives us significance. We avoid looking at the ten areas where we're worse. 

Nobody is thankful for what they have. Nobody appreciates where they live or what they've been given. Everyone feels compelled to point and blame. And they blame by the measure where they had significance — where they made progress, where they're ahead.

The feedback loop: the more you judge, the more you see to judge. The more you focus on accusation, the more accusation you find. It amplifies itself.

What breaks it isn't fixing the behavior. It's not "stop judging." That's another rule. Rules don't break feedback loops.

What breaks it is gratitude. Appreciation BEFORE accusation. Thanks before the finger-pointing. That's the circuit breaker.

This connects to the dehumanization pattern — war requires dehumanizing the enemy so you can do the unthinkable. We're close to that now in the culture. Not with weapons, but with words. With contempt. With the conviction that the other side isn't just wrong but less than.

The deeper layer for the article: weave this in around or just after "Same pattern. Six costumes." Show that the pattern isn't just structural — it's motivational. People judge where they're strong. They hide where they're weak. And the only thing that interrupts the cycle is the same thing that reverses entropy: energy from outside the system. Gratitude is that energy. It comes from recognizing what you've been given, not what you've earned.
