## ① [Physical Law](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/physical+law): [[Information Theory]] ([Oxford Reference](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/information+theory)) ([[Shannon]] ([Oxford Reference](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/shannon)))

At the heart of [[Information Theory]] ([Oxford Reference](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/information+theory)) is [[Shannon]] ([Oxford Reference](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/shannon)) entropy — measuring uncertainty or surprise in a set of outcomes:

$$H = -\sum p(x) \log p(x)$$

The more evenly distributed the probabilities, the higher the entropy (more uncertainty). The more predictable the system, the lower the entropy. Information is what reduces uncertainty — it gives signal in a sea of noise.

[[Shannon]] ([Oxford Reference](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/shannon))'s channel capacity theorem: 

$C = B \cdot \log_2(1 + S/N)$

 — capacity dep

$$H_{\text{spiritual}} = -\sum p(\omega) \log p(\omega)$$

piritual Mirror: Truth as Divine Information

$$H_{\text{spiritual}} = -\sum p(\omega) \log p(\omega)$$

Where $\omega$ = possible spiritual states (choices, beliefs, paradigms) and $p(\omega)$ = belief-weighted likelihood.

Truth is what reduces spiritual entropy — the clarifying signal of God's Word in the noise of human confusion. The less aligned with truth, the more informational entropy carried — more confusion, contradiction, and existential dissonance.

The [[Shannon]] ([Oxford Reference](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/shannon)) mapping in the framework: 

$C = B \cdot \log_2(1 + F/D)$

 where 

$F$
