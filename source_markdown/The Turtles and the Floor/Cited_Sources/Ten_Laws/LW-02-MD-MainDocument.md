## ① [Physical Law](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/physical+law): [[Quantum Measurement]] ([Oxford Reference](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/quantum+measurement)) and Collapse

Quantum systems exist in superpositions — multiple potential states simultaneously — until observed. The act of measurement causes wavefunction collapse:

$$|\Psi\rangle = c_1|\psi_1\rangle + c_2|\psi_2\rangle + \cdots \xrightarrow{\text{observation}} |\psi_k\rangle$$

The probability amplitudes 

$|c_i|^2$

 determine likelihoods, but observation selects one outcome from the full superposition.

Key aspects:
- **Superposition** = multiple simultaneous realities
- **Observation** = collapses potential into a single outcome
- **Probability amplitudes** determine likelihoods but do not determine which outcome actualizes
- Observation causes reality to "choose"
