## ① [Physical Law](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/physical+law): Fluid Dynamics (Navier–Stokes)

The Navier–Stokes equation governs how fluids move:

$$\rho\left(\frac{\partial \mathbf{v}}{\partial t} + \mathbf{v} \cdot \nabla \mathbf{v}\right) = -\nabla p + \mu \nabla^2 \mathbf{v} + \mathbf{f}$$

Where:
- 

$\rho$

: fluid density
- 

$\mathbf{v}$

: velocity field
- 

$p$

: pressure
- 

$\mu$

> [!info]- Spoken T
> [!math] Equation
> $$\rho\frac{\partial \mathbf{v}}{\partial t} = -\nabla P + \mu_{\text{spiritual}} + \mathbf{F}_{\text{divine}}$$

lation
> \mathbf f

: external forces

Flow depends on pressure, resistance, viscosity, and directionality. This governs everything from water to weather systems to blood circulation.
