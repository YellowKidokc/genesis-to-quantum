## ① [Physical Law](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/physical+law): Chaos Theory and Nonlinear Dynamics

In chaotic systems, small changes in initial conditions lead to exponentially different outcomes — the butterfly effect. The Lorenz system:

$$\dot{x} = a(x - y), \quad \dot{y} = bx - y - xz, \quad \dot{z} = xy - cz$$

This system is deterministic but not predictable. Key features:
- **Sensitive dependence** on initial conditions
- **Aperiodic** (non-repeating trajectories)
- **Strange attractors** — fractal geometry in phase space
- **Deterministic chaos**: the system follows laws, but behaves unpredictably
