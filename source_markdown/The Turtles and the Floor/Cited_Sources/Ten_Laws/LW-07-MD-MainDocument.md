## ① [Physical Law](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/physical+law): Cosmological Expansion (Friedmann Equation)

Cosmology studies the origin, structure, and evolution of the universe. The Friedmann equation, derived from [[General Relativity]] ([Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/general-relativity//)):

$$\left(\frac{\dot{a}}{a}\right)^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho - \frac{k}{a^2} + \frac{\Lambda}{3}$$

Where:
- $a(t)$: scale factor — how fast space is expanding
- 

$\rho$

: matter/energy density
- 

$k$

: curvature of space
- 

$\Lambda$

>

constant
