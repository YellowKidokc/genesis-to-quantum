## Design Principles for These Disclaimers

1. **Each one is unique.** No two say the same thing. A reader who encounters multiple articles gets a deepening understanding of the author's position, not a repeated boilerplate.

2. **Each one matches its article.** The disclaimer for the data article (Tangent D) talks about the data. The disclaimer for the neuroscience article (Tangent C) talks about embodied experience. The disclaimer for the Cross article (Art 4) talks about the centrality of the Atonement.

3. **Every disclaimer affirms the same core conviction from a different angle:**
   - The Bible is true (not "contains truth" — IS true)
   - Physics and Scripture describe the same reality
   - This work resolves apparent tensions, not real contradictions
   - The framework doesn't elevate science over Scripture or reduce Scripture to science
   - The author takes both sources at their word and follows where they converge

4. **They build cumulatively.** A reader who reads all twelve disclaimers has encountered the author's full theological and methodological position without ever reading a formal statement of faith. The disclaimers ARE the statement of faith, distributed across the series.

5. **They never apologize.** The tone is not "sorry if this offends" but "here's what I believe and why I'm doing this." Confidence without arrogance. Conviction without combativeness.

6. **They end with the bridge.** Each one closes by pointing at the connection — not arguing for it, just naming it. "The Bible said X. The physics agrees." Let the reader decide what that means.
