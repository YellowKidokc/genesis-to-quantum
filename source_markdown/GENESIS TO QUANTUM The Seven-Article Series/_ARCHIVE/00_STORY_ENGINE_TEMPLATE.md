# GENESIS TO QUANTUM — STORY ENGINE TEMPLATE (v1)

Purpose:
- Make each article repeatable without flattening the voice.
- Keep one central question and answer it through multiple lanes.

## Core Rule
Start with one simple question.
Then answer it in three lanes:
1. Text lane (Scripture/narrative observation)
2. Physics lane (formal mechanism/equation)
3. Evidence lane (experiment/history/data)

If one lane is weak, say it directly in the Audit.

## Standard Article Skeleton
1. **Hook Scene (human stakes)**
   - 3-6 paragraphs.
   - Put the reader inside a moment, not a concept.

2. **The Question**
   - One line.
   - Example: "Why did one measurement collapse reality and the first one did not?"

3. **Claim (provisional)**
   - One paragraph.
   - State what this article argues, without pretending it's final proof.

4. **Lane A: Text Observation**
   - Quote exact textual sequence.
   - Show what is observed before interpretation.

5. **Lane B: Physics Mechanism**
   - Define the formal model.
   - One key equation + plain-language interpretation.

6. **Lane C: Evidence Anchor**
   - One strongest empirical anchor.
   - If historical, use sequence and primary-source posture.

7. **Competing Explanations**
   - 2-4 strongest alternatives.
   - Explain why each is insufficient.

8. **Convergence**
   - Show why all three lanes point to the same structural answer.

9. **Bridge to Next Article**
   - End with the next unresolved question.
   - Make the reader need the next step.

10. **[Falsification Criteria](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/falsification+criteria)**
   - 3-5 concrete kill conditions.

11. **The Audit**
   - What's load-bearing.
   - What's suggestive but incomplete.
   - Where we overreached.

## Section Naming Pattern (recommended)
- The Thread
- §1 Observation
- §2 Mechanism
- §3 Distinctions/Typology
- §4 System-Level Consequences
- §5 Historical/Test Case
- §6 The Inverse or Resolution Path
- The Convergence
- [Falsification Criteria](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/falsification+criteria)
- The Audit

## Length and Pacing Targets
- Total: 3,500-5,500 words
- Hook + Question + Claim: 10-15%
- Main argument lanes: 60-70%
- Convergence + bridge: 10-15%
- Falsification + Audit: 10-15%

## Quality Gate Before Publish
- Can the central question be stated in one sentence?
- Does each lane independently support the claim?
- Is at least one unresolved mechanism clearly named?
- Does the bridge line naturally force the next article?
- Does the Audit read like honesty, not branding?

## One-Line Formula
Question -> Mechanism -> Evidence -> Objection -> Convergence -> Next Question.
