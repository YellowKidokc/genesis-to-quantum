A funnel shape that starts wide at the top and narrows toward the bottom — representing possibility space contracting. The wide opening at top is labeled "N degrees of freedom" in white chalk. The narrow bottom is labeled "N - debt" in red chalk. Along the narrowing walls, small text reads "compound interest" with a downward arrow. Below the funnel: "DEBT" in bold red caps. A funnel shape that starts narrow at the top and widens toward the bottom — representing possibility space expanding. The narrow top is labeled "bound" in dim white chalk. A simple cross symbol drawn in white chalk — not ornate, just two intersecting lines — at the exact point where the left funnel would be at its narrowest (maximum debt) and the right funnel begins its expansion. This is the inflection point. A small label near the cross reads "Maximum debt absorbed" in white chalk.
