## I. What Physics Actually Says About Observers

Let me tell you where mainstream physics stands on this — honestly, without dressing it up.

There are three serious attempts in modern physics to deal with the observer problem. Each one gets closer to what the framework needs. None of them arrives.

**The first attempt is Relational Quantum Mechanics** — developed by physicist Carlo Rovelli in the 1990s. The core idea: there is no observer-independent reality. Every fact about a quantum system is a fact *relative to* a specific observer. What's true for observer A is not necessarily true for observer B. Both are valid. Reality is perspectival.

This sounds like it's heading toward observer hierarchy. It isn't. Rovelli's framework makes all observers equal — facts are relative, but no observer is more right than another. It's perspectivalism without privilege. The framework needs the opposite: observers are unequal, and the inequality is structured, not random.

**The second attempt is Quantum Reference Frames** — formalized in a major 2025 publication. The description of a quantum system changes depending on which reference frame you measure from. Entanglement and superposition are frame-dependent. Two observers in different quantum reference frames can disagree about whether a system is entangled at all.

Closer. Frame matters now. But again — all frames are treated as equally valid. There's no weighting. No hierarchy. No scalar that makes one observer's measurement more physically consequential than another's.

**The third attempt is the Extended Wigner's Friend scenario** — and this is the one that stops you cold.

Rewind. Let me explain Wigner's Friend before we go further.

Eugene Wigner proposed a thought experiment in 1961. His friend is in a sealed lab, measuring a quantum particle. From inside the lab, the friend sees a definite outcome — the particle is spin-up or spin-down. From outside the lab, Wigner hasn't observed anything yet. From his perspective, the entire contents of the lab — particle, measuring device, friend and all — are still in superposition.

Two observers. Same system. Fundamentally different descriptions of what's real.

In 2018, Daniela Frauchiger and Renato Renner extended this to its logical conclusion. They proved that if you allow observers to observe other observers — nested measurement, one inside the other — standard quantum mechanics produces direct logical contradictions. Two observers following all the rules of quantum mechanics can arrive at mutually inconsistent conclusions about what happened.

The physics says: nested observers produce paradoxes. The framework says: nested observers produce hierarchy. That's not the same claim — but it's the same territory. And nobody in physics has a formalism for turning the paradox into a structure.

## II. What Genesis 3 Is Actually Claiming

Now let's be precise about what the framework is actually asserting. Because the claim has to be stated exactly or it becomes unfalsifiable — and an unfalsifiable claim is not a claim, it's a story.

Here's what happens in Genesis 3:6-7.

But the text says: *"Then the eyes of both were opened."*

This is not a story about moral responsibility or federal headship as a theological abstraction. This is a description of observer hierarchy in the measurement process. Two observers with different measurement authority interacting with the same system, producing different scopes of collapse.

No current formalism in physics can describe this. And I want to sit with that for a moment — because the absence of a formalism is not evidence against the claim. It's evidence of a gap. And gaps are where discoveries live.

## III. The Variable Physics Doesn't Have

Here's what I think the framework needs to introduce — not as a proven result, but as a formally proposed extension to measurement theory.

I'm calling it informational fidelity. Notation: $I_f$.

Rewind. Here's the plain version before the math.

Think about the difference between getting instructions directly from the person who designed a system versus getting them secondhand through someone who got them thirdhand from someone who misheard them originally. Even if you're all trying to follow the same instructions, the quality of your coupling to the original specification degrades at every remove. Noise enters. Mediation distorts. Your ability to execute the original intent decreases — not because you're less intelligent or less willing, but because the information channel between you and the source has been compromised.

That's informational fidelity. It's a measure of how clearly you're coupled to the original signal — the system specification, the designer's intent, the Logos encoding.

The formal version:

$$I_f = \frac{S_{\text{direct}}}{S_{\text{direct}} + N_{\text{adversarial}} + D_{\text{mediation}}}$$

Three terms in that denominator. $S_{\text{direct}}$ is the signal strength from direct access to the source. $N_{\text{adversarial}}$ is injected noise — interference introduced by an adversarial process. $D_{\text{mediation}}$ is degradation from secondhand transmission — each layer of remove from the original adds distortion.

Now apply it to Eden.

Adam received the instruction directly. God told him: *"You may freely eat of every tree of the garden, but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat."* Direct transmission. No mediation. No noise at the point of reception. $I_f$ is high.

Eve received the instruction secondhand — through Adam, before the serpent entered — and then had her channel actively compromised. The serpent's first move is pure $N_{\text{adversarial}}$: *"Did God really say...?"* Noise injection. Signal degradation. By the time Eve is making her decision, her $I_f$ is significantly lower than Adam's — not because she is lesser, but because her informational channel to the original specification has been more severely compromised.

Now here's the formal claim.

Measurement authority — the scope of wavefunction collapse an observer can actualize — is not constant across observers. It is a function of two things multiplied together:

$$\text{Authority} = I_f \times \Phi$$

where $\Phi$ is integrated information — a measure of the quality and coherence of the observer's consciousness. The IIT (Integrated [[Information Theory]] ([Oxford Reference](https://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/information+theory))) measure, currently the strongest formal framework for consciousness as a physical variable.

An observer's measurement authority is the product of their consciousness quality and their informational fidelity to the Logos. Both matter. A highly conscious observer with a compromised channel has reduced authority. A highly faithful channel through a low-consciousness observer also has reduced authority. The product is what counts.

And then the collapse threshold.

There exists a value $I_f^*$ such that measurement only actualizes a global state change when the observer's authority exceeds the threshold. Below the threshold: the observer experiences a local result, a personal perception of outcome, but the global wavefunction does not collapse. Above the threshold: the collapse propagates systemically.

Eve's authority was below $I_f^*$. She experienced the fruit. She tasted it. Something happened locally. But the global state — "the eyes of both were opened" — required the second observer whose authority exceeded the threshold.

This is the Eve-Adam asymmetry. Not a theological assertion about male authority. A measurement-theoretic claim about what determines the scope of wavefunction collapse. And it's a claim that generates specific, testable predictions.
