## The Symplectic Structure

In 1809, Joseph-Louis Lagrange discovered that the equations of motion for any physical system can be written in a special form. Position and momentum come in pairs. When position increases, momentum changes in the opposite direction. When momentum increases, position changes in the opposite direction. Opposite signs. Anti-correlation. Built into the geometry.

Hamilton formalized it. The equations now bear his name:

dq/dt = +∂H/∂p
dp/dt = -∂H/∂p

See those signs? Plus and minus. That's the anti-correlation. It's not a feature of any particular system — it's a feature of the geometry that ALL Hamiltonian systems live on. That geometry is called symplectic geometry, and the group of transformations that preserves it is called the symplectic group.

For n pairs of variables, the symplectic group is Sp(2n, ℝ). It acts on a 2n-dimensional space.

For five pairs: Sp(10, ℝ). Ten dimensions. Five conjugate pairs. Anti-correlation built into the geometry by a mathematical theorem — Darboux's theorem — that says any symplectic space can be decomposed into conjugate pairs. Not as a choice. As a necessity.

I designed ten laws from scripture. The symplectic group produces exactly ten dimensions from geometry. Same number. Same pairing. Same anti-correlation.

That's not what stopped me. What stopped me is where the symplectic group leads.
