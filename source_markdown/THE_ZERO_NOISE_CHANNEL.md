# The Zero-Noise Channel

Has there ever been a human layer of abstraction that hasn't drifted?

Language drifts. "Awful" used to mean "full of awe." "Nice" used to mean "ignorant." Give any word three centuries and it means something different. Law drifts. The Constitution means something different in every generation's court — not because the words changed, but because the humans reading them changed. Philosophy drifts. Science drifts — Thomas Kuhn proved it in 1962. Paradigm shifts are the mechanism by which science progresses, and each shift redefines what the previous generation thought was settled.

Theology drifts. That's what denominations are. Two thousand years of people reading the same text and arriving at different conclusions. Not because the text moved. Because the readers did.

Music notation is close, but interpretation varies. Legal codes try, but precedent evolves them. Even formal logic holds only if you accept the axioms — and the axioms themselves are debatable.

Math doesn't drift.

The Pythagorean theorem is exactly as true in Babylon as it is on Mars in 3000 AD. 2+2=4 in every culture, every century, every mind that processes it. The square root of two is irrational in every language that can express the concept. Nobody votes on it. Nobody reinterprets it. Nobody holds a council to decide whether pi is still 3.14159. The output is identical across every instance that runs the computation.

Nothing else humans have ever built can say that.

## Shannon's Insight

In 1948, Claude Shannon founded information theory with a single paper. He proved that every communication channel has a capacity — a maximum rate at which information can be transmitted without error. If you transmit below capacity, you can achieve arbitrarily low error. If you transmit above capacity, errors are inevitable.

The capacity depends on the noise. More noise, less capacity. Zero noise, maximum capacity. A channel with zero noise transmits the signal perfectly — every bit arrives exactly as it was sent.

So here's the question that changes everything: if God's truth doesn't drift — "the same yesterday, today, and forever" — then what is the only human language capable of carrying it without corruption?

The one that also doesn't drift.

Math is the zero-noise channel. Every other carrier — language, philosophy, tradition, even theology as human practice — introduces noise. The signal degrades. The message drifts. Not because the source changes, but because the channel adds its own uncertainty. 2+2=4 doesn't drift because math IS the channel, not just the message. The medium and the content are the same thing.
